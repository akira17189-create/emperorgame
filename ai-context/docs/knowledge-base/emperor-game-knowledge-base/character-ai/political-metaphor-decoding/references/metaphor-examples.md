# Political Metaphor Examples

## Example 1: "Water Ends, Clouds Rise" Poem
- **Source**: Emperor Jiajing's handwritten note
- **Content**: "行到水穷处，坐看云起时" (Walk to where water ends, sit and watch clouds rise)
- **Decoding**:
  - "Water" (水) = Yang Jinshui (杨金水)
  - "Water ends" = Yang Jinshui is insane, investigation dead end
  - "Cloud" (云) = Yun Niang (芸娘)
  - "Clouds rise" = Focus shifts to Yun Niang as new lead
- **Action**: Go to Zhenfusi prison to interrogate Yun Niang

## Example 2: Divine Turtle of Han Emperor
- **Discovery**: Turtle with inscription "汉后元初年戊寅"
- **Age**: 1,730 years old
- **Political Connection**:
  - Han Emperor Wen = virtuous ruler, practiced "non-action"
  - Emperor Jiajing compares himself to Han Emperor Wen
  - Presenting turtle implies Jiajing is also a virtuous ruler
- **Strategy**: Have the heir present it as an auspicious sign (祥瑞)
- **Purpose**: Please Emperor and pave way for Prince's succession

## Example 3: Liubiju Inscription Incident
- **Background**: Original plaque by Yan Song, "必" character changed by Jiajing
- **Hai Rui's Inscription**: "正人心而靖浮言" (Rectify hearts and calm rumors)
- **Political Implications**:
  - Hai Rui recommended by Prince of Yu
  - Yan Song's son executed in May
  - Inscription seen as challenge to Emperor's authority
- **Emperor's Response**: Force Prince to copy and display it, testing loyalty
- **Power Shift**: Chen Hong gains authority over Zhenfusi and Tixingsi
