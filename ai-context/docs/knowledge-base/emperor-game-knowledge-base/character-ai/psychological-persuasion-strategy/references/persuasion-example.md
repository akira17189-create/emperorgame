# Psychological Persuasion Example

## Case: Comforting the Heir About Feng Bao's Departure

### Situation
- The Heir (世子) refuses to let Feng Bao leave
- Feng Bao is being exiled to Chaotian Temple
- The Heir has deep emotional attachment to Feng Bao

### Zhang Juzheng's Persuasion Strategy

#### Step 1: Establish Authority
- **Question**: "Who is the greatest in our Ming Dynasty's world?"
- **Answer**: "Grandfather Emperor is the greatest"
- **Question**: "Who does Grandfather Emperor care about most?"
- **Answer**: "Cares about the Heir most"

#### Step 2: Reframe the Purpose
- **Explanation**: "Grandfather Emperor wants Big Companion Feng to go to Chaotian Temple to learn more skills, then come back to accompany the Heir"
- **Key Message**: This is for improvement, not punishment

#### Step 3: Enforce Compliance
- **Statement**: "The Heir cannot disobey Grandfather Emperor's words"
- **Exchange Logic**: "If the Heir lets him leave fast, he will return fast"

#### Step 4: Physical Intervention
- **Action**: Hug the Heir, press his head against own body
- **Purpose**: Block sight of Feng Bao leaving, provide comfort

#### Step 5: Execute Command
- **Order to Feng Bao**: "You leave now"
- **Promise**: "I will send someone to deliver clothes"

### Result
- The Heir accepts Feng Bao's departure
- Compliance achieved through authority appeal and reframing
- Emotional distress managed through physical intervention
