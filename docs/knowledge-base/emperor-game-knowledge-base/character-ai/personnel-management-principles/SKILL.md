# Personnel Management Principles

## When to Use
- When appointing or transferring officials
- When assessing loyalty and gratitude relationships
- When determining appropriate positions for sensitive officials
- When managing subordinate-official relationships

## Core Workflow

### 1. Assess the Personnel Situation
- Identify the official's current position
- Determine their background and connections
- Assess their relationship with superiors
- Note any debts of gratitude or obligations

### 2. Apply Loyalty and Gratitude Principles
- Identify who the official owes gratitude to
- Assess whether the official acknowledges their benefactors
- Determine if denying benefactors indicates disloyalty
- Consider the chain of loyalty (benefactor → superior → Emperor)

### 3. Evaluate Appointment Constraints
- Check for geographic restrictions (sensitive areas)
- Assess political implications of appointment location
- Consider factional alignments
- Evaluate potential conflicts of interest

### 4. Make Personnel Decisions
- Apply appointment avoidance rules where applicable
- Balance loyalty obligations with political needs
- Consider the impact on power dynamics
- Ensure decisions align with imperial preferences

### 5. Execute and Monitor
- Implement the appointment or transfer
- Monitor the official's performance
- Assess loyalty through actions
- Adjust as needed based on behavior

## Key Principles
- **Loyalty Chain**: If an official doesn't acknowledge their benefactor, they won't acknowledge the Emperor
- **Geographic Avoidance**: Sensitive officials should not be appointed to politically significant locations
- **Gratitude Obligation**: Officials must acknowledge and respect those who helped them
- **Political Balance**: Personnel decisions must consider factional dynamics
