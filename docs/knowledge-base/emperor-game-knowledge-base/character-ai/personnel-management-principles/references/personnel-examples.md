# Personnel Management Examples

## Example 1: Qi Dazhu and Hai Rui
- **Situation**: Qi Dazhu is Emperor's guard, owes life to Hai Rui
- **Principle Applied**: If Qi Dazhu doesn't acknowledge Hai Rui, he won't acknowledge Emperor
- **Actions Required**:
  - Qi Dazhu must visit Hai Rui (his benefactor)
  - Get medical treatment from Li Shizhen (at Hai Rui's home)
  - Remove all guards from Hai Rui's residence (he has no power)
- **Rationale**: Hai Rui is a minor official with no real power, excessive guarding is unnecessary

## Example 2: Hai Rui's Appointment Transfer
- **Original Request**: Hai Rui requested transfer to Fenyi (Yan Song's hometown)
- **Political Risk**: Hai Rui (recommended by Prince) in Yan Song's hometown could provoke conflict
- **Constraints Applied**:
  - Cannot serve in Jiang-Zhe region (tax-heavy area)
  - Cannot serve in Fenyi (Prime Minister's hometown)
- **Final Decision**: Transfer to Xingguo County (200 li from Fenyi)
- **Warning to Prince**: Do not recklessly recommend people to Ministry of Personnel
- **Execution**: Xu Jie sends court letter to Zhao Zhenji

## Key Avoidance Rules
- **Economic Centers**: Avoid appointing controversial officials to tax-heavy regions
- **Political Hometowns**: Avoid appointing officials to hometowns of powerful ministers
- **Sensitive Regions**: Keep controversial officials away from politically significant areas
